Viking Barley Porridge

Nordic barley porridge, known as byggrynsgr√∏t in Norway, is a traditional, slow-cooked dish made from pearl barley simmered in milk until tender and creamy. Naturally nutty and satisfying, this rustic porridge has been a staple in Scandinavian kitchens for centuries, offering both comfort and nourishment with every spoonful.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup pearl barley
  • 4 cups water
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 1/2 cup milk or cream
  • 1/4 cup honey or brown sugar (optional)

Directions

  1. In a large saucepan, combine the pearl barley, water, and salt. Bring to a boil,then reduce the heat to low and simmer for 45-60 minutes, or until the barley is tender and most of the liquid has been absorbed.
  2. Stir in the butter and milk or cream, and continue to cook for an additional 5-10 minutes, or until the porridge reaches your desired consistency. Sweeten with honey or brown sugar, if desired, and serve hot.
